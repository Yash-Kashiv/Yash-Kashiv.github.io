About Me

Hello! I'm Yash, an M.Tech student in Mechanical Engineering at IIT Gandhinagar, currently working at the IIT-Gn Robotics Lab under the guidance of Prof. Harish Palanthandalam-Madapusi, where I'm deeply involved in robotics research focused on dexterous manipulation, motion planning, and control strategies.

A growing part of my research also focuses on Imitation learning, where robots learn new skills by observing demonstrations — a powerful approach to accelerate behavior generalization in complex environments. I'm especially interested in combining classical control frameworks with modern learning approaches to tackle real-world manipulation challenges.

My research work is actively involved with the Advanced Robotics team at Addverb Technologies, contributing to the development of bimanual manipulation capabilities for their humanoid platforms.

To support my work, I use a variety of tools and frameworks, including ROS, Python, RViz, PyTorch, and MuJoCo, allowing me to design, simulate, and test robotic systems in both simulated and real-world environments.

Where I Come From

Growing up in Satwas, a small town in the Dewas district of Madhya Pradesh, has deeply influenced the way I view the world. I completed both my schooling and early education in my hometown, and later pursued my B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering from Jabalpur Engineering College. While resources were limited in my early years, curiosity was never in short supply.

I've always been fascinated by the idea of building things, of understanding how systems work and how they can be made better. My journey into robotics truly began during my undergraduate studies, when I was selected for the B.Tech UG Inbound Program and spent a semester at IIT Indore.

That opportunity opened up a whole new world for me — it was the first time I had access to a research ecosystem that challenged and inspired me in equal measure. It was during that time that I encountered robotics as more than just a concept — I experienced its potential, and I knew I wanted to be a part of it.

Outside of academics, I spend my time working on travel documentaries and photography. I enjoy capturing places, people, and everyday moments — not for presentation, but as a way to document experiences that are meaningful to me. These interests give me a change of pace from research while still letting me explore and learn in different ways.

Projects

01

SPAC-R: Suction-Driven Paper Assembly and Crafting Robot

Designed and developed SPAC-R, a 4-DOF Delta robot for automating paper craft flower assembly, integrating a suction-based pick-and-place system, automated glue application, and inverse kinematics for smooth motion control; powered by an Arduino Nano with custom C++ code for precise actuation and coordination.

Arduino Nano Mechatronic System Integration
02

Mechanical Impedance Analysis in Human Arm During Ball-Balancing Task

Conducted a comparative analysis of mechanical impedance in dominant and non-dominant human arms during a ball-balancing task with external perturbations, using a motion capture system and pulley-based force setup to estimate stiffness, damping, and inertia, with implications for rehabilitation robotics and human-robot interaction.

Python MoCap HRI
03

Novel Investigation on Influence of Laser Marking of Ni-Ti Shape Memory Alloy Towards Actuation Characteristics for Underwater Soft Robotics

Applied laser marking to Ni-Ti SMA wires—validated by surface profilometry—and fabricated both pristine and marked SMA-PDMS composites in custom 3D-printed molds for a soft-robotic turtle limb. Conducted DSC analysis to compare phase-transformation temperatures before and after marking, confirming a clear shift in actuation thresholds. Tested actuation performance under varied voltages and frequencies, demonstrating faster response, greater angular motion, and improved cycle stability in underwater conditions.

Ni-Ti SMA DSC Soft Robotics
04

Optimal Control and Estimation Techniques for Reaction Wheel Inverted Pendulum

Developed a hybrid control framework for a Reaction Wheel Inverted Pendulum (RWIP) combining energy-based swing-up and linear stabilization techniques (Pole Placement, LQR, Leuenberger Observer, and LQG), achieving fast and robust balance control through simulation and performance comparison.

Simulation Optimal Control RWIP
